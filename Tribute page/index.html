Imran Khan

Imran Khan, former Prime Minister of Pakistan, is widely recognized as a charismatic leader, a legendary cricketer, and a
philanthropist. He was born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan. His leadership on and off the field has left a deep
mark on the nation.

As a cricketer, Imran Khan led Pakistan to its first-ever World Cup victory in 1992. His determination and belief inspired
millions. Following his retirement from cricket, he dedicated his life to social welfare, establishing Shaukat Khanum
Memorial Cancer Hospital and Namal University.

In politics, Imran Khan founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party. His vision for a corruption-free and prosperous
Pakistan resonated with the people, ultimately leading him to the position of Prime Minister in 2018.
